Performing academic research on technologies that can be used to enhance or reduce women's safety at UPenn

In the context of our research on the implications of new technologies for women's safety, my role was multifaceted and instrumental in understanding and analyzing the dataset. I began by comprehending the dataset's profile, which laid the foundation for our study. Understanding the nature and composition of the data provided essential context for our subsequent analysis, ensuring a more focused exploration. My expertise in performing exploratory analysis was pivotal. I quantified the accessibility and spread of information related to women's safety technologies, offering valuable insights into the prevalence of this issue and how information and technologies were being disseminated and accessed. Additionally, my evaluation of usability brought a user-centered perspective to our research, ensuring we thoroughly examined the practicality and effectiveness of these technologies in real-world scenarios. One of the standout contributions of my role was conducting interviews for a meta-analysis focused on apps designed to prevent sexual violence against college-aged women. These qualitative interviews added a human dimension to our research, providing deeper insights into user experiences and perceptions surrounding these apps. My comprehensive approach, encompassing both quantitative and qualitative analysis, significantly enriched our research, contributing to a more holistic understanding of the implications of technology on women's safety.
